Robotics

It is the discipline of engineering that studies and develops methods that allow a robot to perform specific tasks by automatically reproducing human work. Although robotics is a branch of engineering, more precisely mechatronics, in it converge approaches of many disciplines of a humanistic nature, such as linguistics, and scientific: biology, physiology, psychology, electronics, physics, computer science, mathematics and mechanics.

Autonomous robots.

Machines capable of sensing, by means of special sensors, the environmental context and decisions relevant to the situation in which they operate. This is possible thanks to various learning systems, including those based on machine learning and artificial intelligence. Unlike non-autonomous robots, the software of an autonomous robot does not acts deterministically, but allows the machine to self-learn thanks to data sensors, in order to adjust its behavior in an increasingly refined.

Not autonomous robots.

These machines are managed by a software that instructs them in every aspect regarding the operations. Alternatively, they can be managed directly by humans using remote control systems. In the first case we find the industrial robots, active on the assembly lines, while in the second case a classic example is constituted by remotely controllable drones, rather than by bomb-disposal robots engaged in inspecting places at risk and defusing any threats that may be life-threatening to humans.

Tesla Bot.

It develops the new frontier of automation, which will also give life to a humanoid two-pedal robot for general use capable of performing unsafe, repetitive or boring tasks.
